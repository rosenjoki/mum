To My Pillars,
The Murimis

From Rose, with every beat of her heart 🤎

MMU Graduation, Class of 2025 🎓 — Esther, James, Rose & Destine
where it all begins

Before anything else, thank you.

There are words that live quietly in a person's chest for years — gratitude too big for a simple hug, love too deep for a text message. This is me finally letting those words breathe. This is me, Rose Njoki Murimi, standing on the other side of one of life's tallest mountains and turning back to say: I did not climb this alone.

You were there. Every single time.

When they said university is hard,
you said, "We've got you."
When fees were due and the world felt heavy,
you opened your hands — and your hearts.
When I was unsure, your prayers
were the wind beneath my feet.
✦ ✦ ✦
for every fee paid

You Funded a Dream

Four years of BBIT at Multimedia University. Four years of tuition, of textbooks, of late-night printing runs, of data bundles, of everything in between. You never once made me feel like a burden. Not once did you sigh and say it was too much — you just made it happen.

Every shilling you sent
was more than money — it was faith.
Faith that I would show up.
Faith that I would finish.
Faith that your sacrifice would bloom.

It bloomed, Dad and Mum.
Look at me now — it bloomed.

That degree? It has your fingerprints on it just as much as mine. The scroll I held in my hands on that stage — it belongs to all of us.

✦ ✦ ✦
for every prayer whispered

You Covered Me in Grace

I don't know exactly when you prayed for me — maybe at 5am before the house woke up, or quietly in church with hands folded and my name on your lips. But I felt it. Even when things got hard at school, there was something carrying me through. That something was you and God answering your prayers.

On the days I almost quit,
your prayers held my knees steady.
On the nights I doubted,
your faith in me was louder
than every voice that said I couldn't.

Thank you for speaking life over me
even when I forgot to speak it myself.

🙏 A Gift I Carry Always

There are inheritances of wealth, and then there are inheritances of spirit. The way you pray — without fear, with full surrender — that is the greatest thing you've handed me. I carry it into every room I walk into.

✦ ✦ ✦
to each of you

Three People, One Love

You are not just family. You are home. Each one of you has shaped me in ways I am only now beginning to understand. Let me look you in the eye — through these words — and tell you what you mean to me.

Mum — Esther
My first teacher. My softest safe place.
"Mum, you are the reason I know what grace looks like. You never ran out of warmth, even when life made things tight. You showed me how to hold a family together with quiet strength, deep love, and a faith that doesn't waver. Every time I think of the word 'home', your face is the first thing I see. Thank you for being my calm in every storm. I love you endlessly."
Dad — James
My rock. My provider. My example.
"Dad, you worked so I could learn. You gave so I could grow. There is a kind of love that doesn't always say 'I love you' out loud — it shows up in school fees, in phone calls checking if I'm okay, in that quiet nod that says, 'I'm proud of you.' I see it. I've always seen it. This degree is as much yours as it is mine. Thank you for believing in a future for me even before I fully believed in it myself."
Destine
My little brother. My unexpected hero.
"Destine, do you know how much joy you bring just by being you? Watching you look at me on graduation day — that look was everything. You make me want to be someone worth looking up to. I hope our journey together shows you that anything is possible when family lifts each other. Your turn is coming, and I will be right there, cheering the loudest. I love you, little one."
✦ ✦ ✦
looking forward together

Oneness — My Next Chapter

I am stepping into a new season now. From student to young adult, from "Rose who is studying" to "Rose who is building." And I want to be honest with you about something — growing up doesn't mean growing apart. I believe in us, in the Murimis, more now than ever.

I don't want to just succeed alone,
I want us to rise together.
What you gave me in love,
I want to give back in kind.

Not because I owe you —
but because giving back to you
is one of the sweetest things
I will ever get to do.

Let's walk this next road as one.

As I step into my career, I carry your values with me. The hard work Dad showed me. The grace Mum lives by. The joy Destine brings without even trying. These are not just memories — they are the tools I'm taking to work every day.

My ask is simple: keep walking with me. A text, a prayer, a laugh on a call — that's all I need. Family is not a season, it's a choice we make every day. And I choose you. Every time.

🤝 A Promise, from me to you

I will make you proud. Not by being perfect — but by being present, being grateful, and never, ever forgetting where I come from. The Murimis made me. And the Murimis will always be home.

forever and always
"His goodness runs after me all the days of my life — and so does yours."
With all the love in my chest and a heart so full it overflows —
Rose Njoki Murimi
